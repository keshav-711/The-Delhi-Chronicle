Echoes of the Past

Discover the seven cities that built modern Delhi.

The Seven Cities

Legend says that Delhi has been built and destroyed seven times. From the mythological Indraprastha of the Mahabharata to the imperial capital of the British Raj, every stone in this city has a story to tell.

The Sultanate Era

(1206–1526)
The time of the Qutub Minar. Various dynasties like the Khiljis and Tughlaqs ruled here, leaving behind massive stone monuments and tombs.

The Mughal Era

(1526–1857)
The Golden Age of architecture. Shah Jahan built the walled city of Shahjahanabad (Old Delhi), the Red Fort, and the Jama Masjid.

The British Era

(1911–1947)
The capital shifted from Kolkata to Delhi. Edwin Lutyens designed "New Delhi" with wide roads, the Parliament, and India Gate.
